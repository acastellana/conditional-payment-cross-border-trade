Cross-border settlement that continues even when the facts are disputed

A Bolivian exporter invoices in BOB.
A Peruvian importer pays in PEN.
The benchmark is locked deterministically.
If shipment timing is contested, GenLayer evaluates the evidence and returns the fact needed for settlement to continue.
Benchmark locking is deterministic.
Shipment disputes are not.
GenLayer is the layer that lets the same trade handle both.

Why GenLayer Matters Here

The distinction that matters

What a normal oracle can do
Lock a benchmark rate and return a number.
What a normal oracle cannot do
Resolve which shipment record controls when real-world evidence conflicts.
What GenLayer does
Evaluates the evidence, returns a machine-usable verdict, and lets the same contract continue automatically.
Shipment Dispute

What happens when timing is contested

Three parallel deployments show how the same contract handles each possible outcome.

What happened
Shipment accepted by the importer. No contest filed.
GenLayer involvement
None needed — settlement proceeds deterministically.
What the contract did
Released 73,950 PEN to the exporter
What happened
Importer contested shipment timing. Both sides submitted customs evidence with conflicting timestamps.
What GenLayer found
TIMELY SHIPMENT
Customs exit receipt confirmed crossing 78 minutes before deadline. Border gate record was a secondary system event.
What the contract did
Released 73,950 PEN to the exporter.
What happened
Importer contested shipment timing. Exporter presented a pre-clearance stamp; importer presented a gate record showing physical crossing four days after deadline.
What GenLayer found
LATE SHIPMENT
Pre-clearance stamp is not a crossing event. Border gate record shows crossing on April 9 — four days after deadline.
What the contract did
Refunded 73,950 PEN to the importer.
What happened
Importer contested shipment timing. Both documents had illegible fields — one couldn't be matched to this shipment, the other had an ambiguous date.
What GenLayer found
UNDETERMINED
Neither document yielded a verifiable crossing timestamp. Evidence insufficient to determine TRUE or FALSE.
What the contract did
Froze 73,950 PEN. 14-day manual review window opened. If no ruling, defaults to refund.
How the System Behaves

Two paths, one contract

Normal case
GenLayer computes the benchmark and the trade settles.
Disputed case
GenLayer evaluates the evidence and returns the fact the contract needs in order to release, refund, or freeze funds.
The benchmark path is deterministic.
The verdict path is not.
That is why GenLayer is necessary.
