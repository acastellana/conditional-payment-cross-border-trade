Trade Lifecycle

From invoice to settlement

Five steps. One contract. If shipment timing is contested, GenLayer resolves the disputed fact — and the same settlement engine continues.

1
Invoice Created
150,000 BOB trade registered on-chain.
2
Benchmark Locked
GenLayer validators agree on 0.493 PEN/BOB.
3
Settlement Computed
150,000 × 0.493 = 73,950 PEN. Awaiting shipment check.
4
Shipment Checked
If uncontested, settlement proceeds. If the importer contests timing, GenLayer evaluates the evidence and returns a verdict.
Shipment accepted. Settlement proceeds to final release.

Importer contested timing. Exporter's customs receipt showed crossing 78 minutes before deadline. Importer's gate record showed 18 minutes after.

TIMELY SHIPMENT
→ Released 73,950 PEN to the exporter.

Importer contested timing. Exporter presented a pre-clearance stamp. Importer's gate record showed physical crossing on April 9 — four days after deadline.

LATE SHIPMENT
→ Refunded 73,950 PEN to the importer.

Importer contested timing. Both documents had illegible fields — one couldn't be matched to this shipment, the other had an ambiguous date.

UNDETERMINED
→ Froze 73,950 PEN. 14-day review window; defaults to refund.
5
Final Outcome
Settled. 73,950 PEN transferred to exporter.

Cancelled. GenLayer found shipment late. 73,950 PEN returned to importer.
Frozen. Evidence insufficient. 73,950 PEN locked. Defaults to refund after 14 days.
Why GenLayer Matters Here

The distinction that matters

Locking the rate is not the hard part. The hard part is when the trade cannot proceed because evidence is contested.

What a normal oracle can do
Lock a benchmark rate and return a number.
What a normal oracle cannot do
Resolve which shipment record controls when real-world evidence conflicts.
What GenLayer does
Evaluates the evidence, returns a machine-usable verdict, and lets the same contract continue automatically.
The benchmark path is deterministic.
The verdict path is not.
That is why GenLayer is necessary.
