The Problem

Why this matters

  • Cross-border invoices often involve two currencies.
  • Payment dates move. FX rates move.
  • Each party often computes the settlement separately, leading to disagreements about which FX rate applies.

GenLayer produces one shared settlement result everyone can reference.

How It Works

What the system actually does

1
Record the invoice
The contract stores the trade obligation.
2
Lock the benchmark
Validators agree on a reproducible FX rate.
3
Adjust if the trade changes
Partial shipments or payment delays update settlement terms.
4
Final settlement
The agreed result becomes the canonical settlement state.
What this is not
✕ Letter of credit ✕ Bank payment rail ✕ Trade document platform ✕ Cargo dispute arbitration
It is a coordination layer for FX settlement of cross-border invoices.
