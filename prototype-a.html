The Story
01 / The Setup

Quinoa export: Bolivia to Peru

A Bolivian exporter invoices 150,000 BOB for a bulk shipment. The Peruvian importer agrees to settle in PEN based on the exchange rate at the time of contract funding.

This isn't just a theoretical model—it's a real trade executed on-chain.

The Friction

Then the shipment is contested

The goods arrive, but the parties disagree on the date. The exporter claims "on time." The importer claims "late." Traditional smart contracts would stall here.

03 / Resolution

GenLayer classifies the outcome

The "Internet Court" evaluates the shipping evidence (dockets, sensor logs, photos) and returns a verdict. The contract then applies the pre-agreed remedy.

04 / The Technology

Why GenLayer?

Smart contracts are great at math, but blind to the world. GenLayer provides the bridge for non-deterministic facts.

By using Intelligent Contracts, we can combine mathematical certainty with human-like judgment for complex disputes.

Deterministic
Locking an FX benchmark. Any oracle can return a number.
Non-Deterministic
Classifying a shipment date from conflicting PDFs and logs.
